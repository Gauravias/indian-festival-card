Indian Festival

Holi is a Hindu festival

that celebrates the beginning of spring, rebirth, and rejuvenation

It's celebrated in many states of India and Nepal, as well as in other South Asian countries and the diaspora.

Diwali, also known as Deepavali

five-day festival that celebrates the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance.

It is India's largest and most important holiday, and is celebrated by Hindus, Jains, Sikhs, and some Buddhists. The festival takes place every autumn between October and November, with the date changing every year.

Maha Shivaratri

Hindu festival that honors the god Shiva and is celebrated in February or March

It is also known as the "Great Night of Shiva" and is observed on the 14th day of the first half of the lunar month of Phalguna. The festival is steeped in Hindu mythology and has deep cultural and spiritual significance.
